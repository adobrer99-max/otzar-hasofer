# Otzar Ha'Sofer — The Treasury

A digital companion and reference guide for the Otzar Ha'Sofer physical folio and Derekh Eretz (22-letter) deck. Not a fortune-telling tool — a reference for the Scribe during a reading, plus a generator for each participant's **Herald**, a procedurally-rendered heraldic sigil built from the three letters drawn in their reading.

The interface is an illuminated-manuscript design that reads on two grounds — the default **charcoal field** (gold linework on deep slate) and a light **vellum** parchment mode, toggled by the sun/moon seal in the header and remembered per browser. The Herald and Mizbe'ach always keep their charcoal field in either mode, an illuminated plate set upon the page. A sticky header (with a grouped "The Guide" menu) collapses to a mobile drawer, and every surface is responsive down to the phone — a companion in the Scribe's pocket.

## What's here

- **Reference guide** (`/guide/*`) — Foundations, all 22 Hebrew letters (one chapter each), Shoresh (the root/Nikkudot mechanic), Derekh Ha'Dorot (the second deck: 7 patriarchal Houses of 8 fully-drafted episode cards plus 7 matriarchal Houses of 16 episodes — 168 entries embodying lived biblical history, unfolding pillar-by-pillar through the Seven Encounters), the Scribe's ritual steps, Sacred Time (festival overrides plus Immediate/Personal/Covenantal time), the Seven Encounters of Bereshit, and the Visual Canon.
- **The Mizbe'ach** (`/guide/mizbeach` to learn it; `/mizbeach` to use it) — the ritual folio, rendered digitally as two procedural SVG pieces: a central panel (Hand Anchor, the three letters, Three Gates, Three Wells, Veiled Anchor, and Tree of Life, in ritual order) and a live ring mandala that reflects the reading's Hebrew date via the Sacred Time engine — an outer Ring of the Month (the twelve Hebrew months, named upright, current month or active festival lit), an eight-phase Ring of the Moon, a Ring of the Weekday (the seven days with Shabbat crowning the top and a persistent gold rim), and a Sabbath Core at the center, bordered by the Shivat HaMinim (seven species) and PaRDeS corners, with a fixed Mizrach vector. **The interactive Mizbe'ach at `/mizbeach` is an operable surface**: the Scribe conducts a whole reading on the folio — read the Hand Anchor, place the three letters and the veiled anchor by clicking a zone and picking, choose the dominant middah on the Tree of Life, and turn the cyclewheels (drag or arrow-key sliders) to set the sacred time, which resolves the festival and spread. "Seal the Reading" produces the participant's Herald through the **same** reading model and `addLayer` the form uses, so a folio reading appears in `/herald`'s history and synthesis automatically. The reference page keeps the Or HaGanuz reveal toggle and SVG/PNG export.
- **The Herald** (`/herald`) — an input form mirroring the reading's ritual flow, auto-detecting today's Hebrew date/Omer/festival (with a backdate override), and a deterministic procedural SVG renderer. The headline **Herald is a synthesis of the participant's first seven readings** — it forms progressively (a "forming — k of 7" state) and is **revealed at the seventh reading**, then stays fixed as canon (later readings are kept as history but never alter it). The synthesis is derived deterministically from the seven readings: the recurring letters across all draws become the shield's charges (the veiled fourth letter of each reading stays hidden, recorded but never rendered); each reading lights its Seven-Encounters Sefirah so a revealed Herald shows the whole lower Tree of Life illuminated with the dominant middah brightest; geography and festival accents accrete; and the dominant three letters are resolved as a Hebrew root through the 4-tier Shoresh hierarchy (Canonical Root → Canonical Name → Related Correspondence → Shoresh Nistar/Hidden Root). At the seventh reading the participant also receives their **Heraldic Epithet**: the Treasury proposes one from the seven readings, the Scribe may reword it, and once sealed it is inscribed into the revealed Herald. The Herald is struck as a **foil-stamped coat of arms** — drawn to be pressed in gold on the back of the recipient's own card deck, so it is flat metal and flat heraldic tinctures with crisp linework, no gradients or texture. It is **coloured from the letters actually drawn**: each letter carries its own association (an element, a planet, or a zodiac sign), so each field is tinctured in its letter's colour (deepened to a jewel tone) while the charges are struck in gold over them, and when a Mother letter is drawn its **element casts the whole field** toward its hue — a fire reading burns on deep red, a water reading cools to deep blue, and no two Heralds share a field. The letterforms are drawn from committed David Libre outlines (so SVG export is font-independent), framed by a full achievement: a gold torse **crest** bearing the reading's zodiac **constellations**, the planet **sigils** standing at the base, a **mantling** of the Shivat HaMinim (the seven species — set by whichever lower Sefirah wins across the Seven Encounters), a Land/Galut **compartment**, an engraved **orle** of gold lozenges, a **semé** of faint estoiles, the reading's **gematria** in Hebrew numerals, and a **motto scroll** carrying the epithet — with nothing ever laid over a letter. Each letter also has a **heraldic charge** — a symbol language of the aleph-bet drawn from each letter's ancient pictographic origin (aleph the ox, mem the water/fountain, zayin the sword, ayin the eye, shin the flame, tav the cross, through all 22) — and a Herald can wear the charges instead of the letterforms. It is derived first ("recognized, never assigned"), and the Scribe may then **curate** it — the device (letterform or charge), the metal (gold/antique/silvered), the flanking species, and which heraldic elements are shown — with a live preview, sealed per participant and synced. Each individual reading is still kept on its own — a history scrubber (with a leading "◆ The Herald" chip for the synthesis) lets you view any single reading's own render, alongside SVG/PNG export, a **written blazon** export (a deterministic, human-readable description of the arms with a JSON appendix — the brief for an illustrator or foil die), a **"Copy image prompt"** that turns the blazon into a rich illustration prompt for an image model (paste into ChatGPT/DALL·E to render an illuminated ketubah-style plate), and, per participant, a Hebrew Birthday and Yahrzeit tracker that surfaces past readings on the same recurring Hebrew date.
- **Commentaries** (`/commentaries`) — the Treasury's Drash tier: Scribe-authored commentaries attached to any letter, Ha'Dorot card, or Hebrew root, dated in Hebrew time and kept alongside the received seed commentary (the doc's "Commentary on ה — Ot Ha'Neshema" by Aleph Yud). Commentaries surface inline on letter chapters, House pages, and in a reading's caption when its three letters match a commented root.
- **The Shelf of Sefarim** (`/sefarim`) — a Beit Midrash presenting the doc's named Books as a visual shelf of leather spines. Each Book that lives here opens as a faithful four-tier commentary page after the doc's Vocabulary Treasury — **Peshat** (the Letter, literal), **Remez** (the Tradition), **Drash** (the Scribe's received and ongoing commentaries, addable inline), **Sod** (the Participant's lived experience). Built richly this pass: **Sefer HaShorashim** (browse the BDB/Strong's root lexicon → read any root through the four tiers, with its Drash layer shared with reading-time root commentaries), **Balagan HaOtzar** (גניזת האוצר — the scribe's genizah: marginal notes, unresolved questions, variant traditions, corrections, discarded hypotheses, and respectful disagreements), and **the Vocabulary Treasury** (an explainer that teaches the four tiers with a live worked example — the letter Heh). Sefer HaMo'adim and the Commentaries link to their existing chapters; Sefer HaDikduk (grammar of roots) keeps its place on the shelf as forthcoming. Authoring extends the same local commentary store (no new database version).

- **The Scriptorium** (`/scriptorium`, unlinked — reachable by URL only) — a drafting studio for the Treasury's own still-first-draft content. Browse every authorable gap (festival contemplative questions, the 112 thin matriarchal Ha'Dorot cards, liturgy English + Sod, the Encounter questions, the Epithet honorifics/emblems, and the 22 letter meanings), write into a form, and watch the **real** component render live as you type (the same `FestivalCard`, Ha'Dorot card, and PaRDeS tiers a reader sees). Drafts save locally (IndexedDB, never synced); the durable artifact is the JSON you export and fold back into `src/data/*.ts`. It only reads the shipped data — nothing drafted here leaks into the live pages until it is committed.

- **Command palette** (press **⌘K / Ctrl-K**, or the magnifier in the header) — a jump-to-anything search over the whole catalogue: the guide/practice/library pages, all 22 letters (by name or Hebrew glyph), the fourteen Houses of the Dorot, and the Sefarim. Grouped, keyboard-driven (↑/↓, Enter, Esc), ranked prefix-first over a static in-memory index (no database read). It indexes titles, names, and keywords — the jump targets — not body prose.

Readings also draw from **Derekh Ha'Dorot** where the ritual calls for it: in Galut, three cards are recorded beneath the three open letters; on Sukkot the Scribe additionally performs the Council of Sefirot (one card); and on Tisha B'Av the 22-Letter deck is ritually locked and the Galut deck's three draws are required (letters stay recorded — the Treasury remains a faithful record). Drawn cards surface their episode/practice/question in the reading's caption, leave small copper Pillar-marks near the Herald's base (accreting into the synthesis), and the card picker softly reflects the Seven Encounters' unfolding — Houses not yet opened are labeled, never barred. Two festival spreads reshape the letters themselves: **Tu Bishvat** becomes the Etz Chaim's vertical four-card draw through the Four Worlds (with a fifth card sealed for the world to come, and PaRDeS taking precedence over the Shoresh), and on **Tu B'Av** the veiled anchor is unveiled and the four letters are read as two pairs seeking only synthesis.

**Sacred Time is complete**: alongside the festival overrides, the Treasury computes the weekly **Torah portion** (parsha) with a homegrown, MIT-clean engine — doubled portions, festival displacement, and the Land/Galut schedule divergence included (cross-check a published luach before ritual reliance). **Personal Time** tracks every anniversary the doc names — Hebrew Birthday, Yahrzeit, wedding, Bar/Bat Mitzvah, Bris, conversion (a grafting — nothing erased), and Aliyah (after which the reading begins with the Letters alone). **Covenantal Time** is lived at `/covenant`: marriage links two participants in a union whose shared **Covenantal Herald** is derived from both partners' Heralds (impaled per pale, in the old heraldic manner) and grows through the seven recorded days of the Sheva Brachot, Chesed to Malchut. Yizkor remains what it is: no reading, only remembrance.

The library now holds **Sefer HaTefillot** (traditional public-domain liturgies by occasion, each a four-tier PaRDeS page with addable Drash) and a real **Sefer HaDikduk** (the classical weak-radical root grammar, applied mechanically to the lexicon). The Herald quietly weaves a participant's Hebrew name into its border geometry (never displayed), animates once at the seventh reading's reveal, and names the **Word of the Life** when the synthesis's dominant letters spell a root. **Physical card art uploads through the app itself**: the Scriptorium's "The Card Art" studio lists all 190 slots (22 letters + 168 Dorot cards); a signed-in Scribe picks a slot, chooses the scan (downscaled client-side), writes the alt text and credit, and publishes — the image lands in a public Supabase Storage bucket, its registry row goes live for **every** visitor instantly (no redeploy), and installed PWAs cache viewed art for offline.

Small graces around the practice: the folio and its guide page carry a **Mizrach finder** — one tap computes your true great-circle bearing to Jerusalem from wherever you stand ("Mizrach from here: 54° — northeast"), the engraved arrow staying a fixed symbol; the ceremonial deck deal can be **heard** (a soft synthesized tick + haptic per card, muted by default, toggled on the reveal); a Today-panel observance opens straight to **that participant's** Herald; and the ⌘K palette now also finds your own **commentaries and Scriptorium drafts**.

Everything is **local-first**: participant, Herald, union, and commentary data lives in the browser (IndexedDB) and the whole app works offline as a PWA. A deployment may optionally enable **the Scribes' Cloud** — accounts (email + password with verification, plus forgot/reset and change-password flows, or an emailed magic link) with private cross-device sync — by configuring a Supabase project (two env vars; see `DEPLOY.md` and `supabase/schema.sql`). Each Scribe's Treasury is locked to their account by Postgres row-level security; without configuration the app runs pure-local, exactly as before, and the Account page ("The Scribe's Seal") explains as much. With the cloud on, **Scriptorium drafts sync** across the Scribe's own devices too, a Scribe may **remove their account** (a cascading, self-scoped deletion that leaves the local Treasury intact), and — the practice's other half — each participant can be handed a **share link**: a public, read-only page (`/shared/:token`) rendering the *derived, veil-free* Herald (never the readings, never the Sod), published as a snapshot, republishable after new readings, and revocable at once. The token is the whole secret — there is no anonymous row listing; a `security definer` RPC reads a single Herald only when given the exact token. Hosting is a static build — `DEPLOY.md` walks through Cloudflare. The user-authored content passes (festival contemplative questions, matriarchal card enrichment, liturgy English polish, and the rest) now have a home — the Scriptorium above, where they can be drafted against a live preview and exported. Still out of scope: participant *accounts* (share links are read-only tokens, not logins), realtime/live-updating shares, "moments of calling," auto-merging Scriptorium drafts into the data files (the fold-in stays a deliberate step), and the physical card art itself.

### A note on licensing

Hebrew calendar conversion uses [`jewish-date`](https://www.npmjs.com/package/jewish-date) (MIT). The more complete `@hebcal/core` library was deliberately avoided — it's GPL-2.0, which isn't safe to bundle into a commercial client-side app. Holiday/Omer/Yahrzeit logic is this project's own thin layer on top of the MIT conversion primitive. Similarly, the Shoresh root data is generated from the public-domain Brown-Driver-Briggs (BDB) lexicon via OpenScriptures — never from HALOT, which is modern-copyrighted. The optional cloud uses [`@supabase/supabase-js`](https://www.npmjs.com/package/@supabase/supabase-js), whose entire dependency tree is MIT.

## Development

```sh
npm install
npm run dev       # local dev server
npm run build     # production build (also generates the PWA service worker)
npm run test      # vitest — includes a determinism test for the Herald renderer
npm run export:folio   # write a print-ready Mizbe'ach folio master (self-contained SVG)
```

`npm run export:folio [outfile.svg]` renders the folio (the ring mandala beside
the central panel) into a single "open folio" spread for physical production: a
**self-contained SVG** with the brand palette baked in as literal hex and the
David Libre + Frank Ruhl Libre fonts embedded as base64, so it scales to any
size and opens correctly at a print shop with no external assets. It renders the
mandala in its **neutral master** state (no live date/festival highlight, which
is a screen-only affordance). Default output: `mizbeach-folio-print.svg` at the
repo root (git-ignored).

Built with Vite, React, and TypeScript. Installable as an offline-capable PWA.
